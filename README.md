## ðŸ“˜ Overview
This repository contains materials from the **Scenario-Based Cybersecurity Laboratory Series**, a course collaboratively developed between **Engineering and Law** programs at Cleveland State University.  
The course, named as **Hack Lab**, immerses students in **realistic cyber-attack and defense scenarios** while simultaneously addressing the **legal, ethical, and policy implications** of each incident.

The goal is to bridge the gap between **technical cyber defense** and **cyber law practice**, cultivating cross-disciplinary professionals capable of identifying, analyzing, and responding to incidents in compliance with both security standards and legal frameworks.

---

## ðŸŽ¯ Learning Objectives
After completing these modules, participants will be able to:
1. Conduct technical analysis of simulated attacks such as DDoS, phishing, and ransomware using open-source tools.  
2. Apply digital forensic principles to collect, preserve, and analyze evidence in line with legal requirements.  
3. Evaluate incident scenarios through the lenses of **policy, regulation, and liability**.  
4. Collaboratively develop incident response reports integrating technical mitigation and legal justification.  
5. Explain how organizational governance, privacy laws, and regulatory frameworks shape cyber defense operations.

## âš™ï¸ Tools and Environment
- **Operating Systems:** Kali Linux, Windows XP/7, Ubuntu Server  
- **Network Tools:** Wireshark, Nmap, Metasploit, Snort, pfSense  
- **Scenario Platforms:** VirtualBox / OCRI / XP Cyber Range  
- **Policy Tools:** NIST 800-61 (Incident Handling), NIST 800-171 (Controlled Unclassified Information)  
- **Legal Frameworks Discussed:** CFAA, ECPA, GDPR, and U.S. breach notification laws
